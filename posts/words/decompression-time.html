decompression time

I pride myself on having a very uncomplicated life. It works that way most of the time, but returning home has not been without its challenges. Everyone is so high-octane and hypercompetitive and equally performative. I miss my friends from all over.. I miss my life. I'm home but I'm not really home because my heart isn't here anymore. I take a scooter on the way to work and pass by the Capitol and I feel a moment of fleeting sentimental affection for the city I once matured in but no longer feel tethered to. I don't think I want my son growing up here either.

Peripheral musings aside, I spent the bulk of my day reading three very interesting papers and writing a research proposal. I had an Italian club sandwich and a cookie for lunch and skipped dinner. I saw my brother and talked to him about his plans for college next year. I also had a rather visceral exchange with my ex about our son as he continues to leverage our son to extort money from me. It's funny, you tell people there's a 30-year age gap and they look at you with sanctimonious judgment like you're a gold digger. If only they knew that he was coming after my assets and not the other way around. I am just trying to survive this vindictive campaign. I'm honestly an idiot because for that age gap, I could have married a rich guy but no no no, me and the fucking need to court and be courted by intellectuals. I don't even feel sorry for myself because I did it against everyone's advice. I wish he would find someone, fall in love, maybe have a new family, and just relinquish the anger and move on with his life as I did with mine. I don't love him nor do I hate him. I feel absolutely nothing for him.
