late nights in dc

The city looks different after midnight. Not worse, not better — just honest. The monuments stop performing and just exist. The streets empty out and you can hear your own footsteps for once.

I took the long way home last night. Down M Street past all the closed shops, across the bridge where you can see the whole river reflecting the city lights. There was nobody else out there. Just me and the cold air and that particular kind of silence that only exists in cities at 2am.

I've been here two years now and I still find new things. A side street I never noticed. A view I'd walked past a hundred times without looking up. Maybe that's the thing about living somewhere — you don't really see it until you stop trying to.

Tomorrow I'll go back to being busy. But tonight, I'm just here. And that's enough.
